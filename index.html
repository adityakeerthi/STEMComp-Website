What is STEMComp?

STEMComp is Waterloo’s biggest student-run STEM (science, technology, engineering and mathematics) competition. Our goal is to give high school students the opportunity to dive deep into the STEM fields that will drive our world forward. STEMComp 2020 is being hosted online by Sir John A. Macdonald Secondary School. This year, we're challenging students across the WRDSB to explore their passion for STEM through creative problem-solving. Compete individually, or with a partner!

Your Challenge

Find a Problem

Think of a problem: either one that occurs within the field of STEM, or one that could be solved using STEM. It can be any problem, big or small!

Find a Solution

Think of a way you could use STEM to help solve this problem. The solution doesn't have to be revolutionary or perfectly refined. Aim to have a unique idea that takes a step in the right direction.

Make a Project

Create a project that puts your solution in action. The type of project is up to you! It could be an experiment, a study, something you built, a computer program—anything in the field of STEM.

Make a Video

Make a 5-minute (maximum) video that shows off your project. Tell us the problem you looked at, and walk us through your project and how it helps solve the problem. Show us all aspects of your project, including areas of improvement for the future, and make sure your video is engaging for all audiences.

How to Compete

1

Register and create your account. If you're working with a partner, make one account and add your partner's information through it.

2

Indicate your project category.

Mathematical Sciences and Technology: your project primarily deals with engineering, computer science, mathematics or physics.

Life Sciences: your project primarily deals with pure biology, chemistry, psychology or medicine.

3

Log in and submit your video by July 1, 11:59 p.m.

4

After the video submission due date, you'll be given two other videos to peer evaluate. Log into your account, watch both videos, and rate them carefully for each judging criteria. Peer judging must be completed by July 3, 11:59 p.m.

5

All video submissions will be posted once the competition is over. Winners will be announced in mid-July.

Judging Criteria

STEMComp will include two rounds of judging. First, videos will be peer judged by other competitiors. Then, the top 15 videos will be scored by our judging panel. Your submission will be scored based on the following criteria:

Interest

Your video is engaging and the content is clear for your audience.

Difficulty

You challenged yourself to dive deep into the complexities of your topic.

Impact

Your project helps solve your problem and helps make a difference.

Sources

Your video gives credit to resources that helped significantly guide your work.

Note: to be eligible for judging, you must be a WRDSB high school student and your video must not exceed 5 minutes.
